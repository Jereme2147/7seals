    Basic Class Structure

  • WhiteBorad Session

    • Just a quick briefing of what to expect from the wod and the timeline. You don't have to spend a pile of time here.

  • Activation

    • This is optional, but a lot of athletes including myself really benefit from this. Just spend some time before a run or even a row and get the muscles moving and joints loose.

  • General Warm up

    • General warm up should include dynamic stretches, target muscle activation, and getting the heartrate up.

  • Specific Warm up

    • This is (in my opinion the most important AND the most frequently left out and skipped over) an important part. This is the part where you spend time demonstrating each movement as well as practicing all of them.

    • This is the time you'll use figure out how to scale for people. Do they need to scale weight or the movement it self?

    • Every movement should be addressed. For example: Cleans. Begin with Burgener warm up or a variation of using pvc. Repeat with a barbell. Then some weight. By the time the wod starts, everyone should be sweating.

    • This can also be in two parts. A quick specific prior to a strength component, then another prior to the metcon.

  • Wod

    • Just before the wod begins, make sure everyone is clear on the wod. Make sure you're comfortable with everyone's scale. Pee break.

  • Cool Down

    • This is one often overlooked. I am very guilty of this. But, it's important. Use a planned cool down for more than one purpose.

    1. An actual cool down.
    2. People who are pressed for time can be sure to get out in time.
    3. It's a buffer for the accidental class run over.
